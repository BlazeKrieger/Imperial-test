DESCRIPTION:

MATERIALS AND APPARATUS:

• Isopropanol (100%)
• Water bath at 50°C
• Buffer PE with 96-100% ethanol (see bottle label for volume)
• Centrifuge at 13,000 rpm
• Scalpel
• Buffer QG

PROCEDURE:

Steps:

1. Excise the DNA fragment from the agarose gel with a clean, sharp scalpel.

2. Weigh the gel slice in a colorless tube. Add 3 volumes Buffer QG to 1 volume gel (100 mg gel ~100 μl).

NOTE: The maximum amount of gel per spin column is 400 mg. For >2% agarose gels, add 6 volumes Buffer QG.

3. Incubate at 50°C for 10 min (or until the gel slice has completely dissolved). Vortex the tube every 2–3 min to help dissolve gel. After the gel slice has dissolved completely, check that the color of the mixture is yellow (similar to Buffer QG without dissolved agarose).

NOTE: If the color of the mixture is orange or violet, add 10 μl 3 M sodium acetate, pH 5.0, and mix. The mixture turns yellow.

4. Add 1 gel volume isopropanol to the sample and mix.

5. Place a QIAquick spin column in a provided 2 ml collection tube. Apply the sample to the QIAquick column and centrifuge for 1 min. Discard flow-through and place the QIAquick column back into the same tube.

NOTE: For sample volumes >800 μl, load and spin/apply vacuum again.

6. If DNA will subsequently be used for sequencing / in vitro transcription / microinjection, add 500 μl Buffer QG to the QIAquick column and centrifuge for 1 min. Discard flow-through and place the QIAquick column back into the same tube.

7. To wash, add 750 μl Buffer PE to QIAquick column and centrifuge for 1 min. Discard flow-through and place the QIAquick column back into the same tube.

NOTE: If the DNA will be used for salt-sensitive applications (e.g., sequencing, bluntended ligation), let the column stand 2–5 min after addition of Buffer PE. Centrifuge the QIAquick column in the provided 2 ml collection tube for 1 min to remove residual wash buffer.

8. Place QIAquick column into a clean 1.5 ml microcentrifuge tube.

9. To elute DNA, add 50 μl Buffer EB to the center of the QIAquick membrane and centrifuge the column for 1 min. Add 30 μl Buffer EB to the center of the QIAquick membrane again, let the column stand for up to 4 min, and then centrifuge for 1 min.

NOTE: If purified DNA is to be analyzed on a gel, add 1 volume of Loading Dye to 5 volumes of purified DNA. Mix the solution by pipetting up and down before loading the gel.

DESCRIPTION:

MATERIALS AND APPARATUS:

• 2.5g yeast extract
• 5g peptone
• 5g NaCl
• 7.5g agar-agar
• 1L sterile bottle (check to see if this fits in the microwave for reheating)
• Sterile dH2O
• Sterile Petri dishes (Hackspace should have ~100mm diameter, holds ~20mL agar, should be able to see 100-200 colonies)
• Ice bucket for keeping antibiotic on
• Chloroamphenicol antibiotic stored at 1000X concentration, dissolved in EtOH
o Recommended stock conc = 25mg/mL, working concentration = 25ug/mL

PROCEDURE:

Per 500mL molten LB agar: (25 plates)

NOTE: This makes 25 plates, but we only need 16 plates total for Stage 1.

1. Weigh out the following into a 1L sterile bottle (20g total, pre-mixed powder in Hackspace contains first three components, then add 7.5g agar-agar separately):
• 2.5g yeast extract
• 5g peptone
• 5g NaCl
• 7.5g agar-agar
2. Make up solution to 500mL using sterile dH2O, gently swirl to mix and minimise amount of powder left on sides of bottle.

3. Screw lid on bottle loosely and label with autoclave tape.

4. Autoclave at 121C for 20 minutes

5. Remove solution from autoclave and cool to 50C (just about hot to touch but not too hot).

NOTE: Don’t need to use water bath for cooling to specific temperature unless generating a lot of plates & need to stop the agar from solidifying.

6. If making LB-Chlor plates, add 500uL of 25mg/mL antibiotic to solution and swirl to mix.

7. Pour 20mL of molten LB agar per 10cm diameter Petri dish. This should be conducted aseptically in a fume hood. Use 25mL pipette with Pipetboy to measure exact volumes.

NOTE: Do not eject volume from pipette too quickly, or bubbles will be introduced. Bubbles can be removed using a small pipette tip. When pouring, remove lid to dish and remove lid to bottle, then pour as specified above. Work quickly, as the agar will harden very quickly so pouring should be done in 5 minutes.

8. Place the lids on the plates and allow them to cool for 45 minutes or until solidified, then invert plates. To avoid condensation on the plates, stack 4-5 plates together, then stack the stacks so that the only plates that will accumulate condensation on the lid are the ones at the top of the stack. Store sealed with Parafilm at 4C.

NOTE: Dried plates should be taped shut and stacked upside down on their lids in a container to protect from light. Write the date of preparation on the plates, make sure plates are not more than 2 months old.

DESCRIPTION:

MATERIALS AND APPARATUS:

• Sterile distilled water
• 10% glycerol
• Graduated cylinder
• Weighing Scale/Boats
• Microcentrifuge tubes (1.5mL eppys)
• Hackspace-provided Eppendorf 5810R Centrifuge
o Manual for use, see Section 5.
• Fume hood for aseptic technique
• 1L sterilised bottle
• Ice bucket
• 1mL cuvettes
• Spectrophotometer
• 4x 50mL Falcon tubes stored at –20C.
• Sterile toothpick or Gilson pipette tip
• Dry ice/liquid nitrogen bath

SOB (Super Optimised Broth)

NOTE: If available in dry premixed form in the Hackspace, check ratios of each component.

• 2% (w/v) tryptone or peptone
• 0.5% (w/v) yeast extract
• 10 mM NaCl
• 2.5 mM KCl
• 20 mM MgSO4

Per 500mL:

1. Dissolve the following in 400mL of distilled water (ddH2O) in a 1L bottle:
• 10g tryptone or peptone
• 2.5g yeast extract
• 0.292g NaCl
• 0.093g KCl
• 1.2g MgSO4

2. Bring final volume to 500mL with more distilled water (ddH2O). Adjust to pH 7.5 before use, requires 1M NaOH solution, pipette 1mL at a time.

10% (v/v) glycerol

• Dilute 1 volume of molecular biology-grade glycerol in 9 volumes of sterile pure H2O.
• Sterilise the solution by passing it through a pre-rinsed 0.22um filter.
• Store in 200mL aliquots at 4C.

PROCEDURE:

NOTE: All steps should be carried out aseptically in the fume hood, unless otherwise stated, keep all reagents on ice, time ~ 55 min total.

Day 1:

1. Streak out stock of previously-competent TOP10 cells onto an LB plate with no antibiotics to obtain single colonies (if LB plate needs to be made, see this protocol for setting plates). Cover with lid and incubate overnight at 37C.

2. Autoclave:
2.1. 500mL SOB medium (not going to use it all)
2.2. 10% glycerol
2.3. Microcentrifuge tubes
2.4. 50mL Falcons (for centrifuging)

Day 2:

1. Pre-warm: Pre-warm 50mL SOB medium at 37C in a water bath at the start of day 2.

2. Reserve control: Keep 10mL SOB medium WITHOUT bacteria in a small bottle or Falcon to act as blank sample when measuring OD600 on Day 3.

3. Starter culture: At end of day, prepare 50mL sterile Falcon containing 10mL SOB medium. Using a sterile toothpick or pipette tip, pick up a single colony of cells from incubated Day 1 plate, then drop into Falcon. Cover flask with foil and incubate this starter culture overnight at 37C in a rotary shaker set to 225rpm. Prewarm the SOB medium in the same incubator, to be used the following day.

NOTE: To grow, bacteria flasks should never be filled with more than a quarter of its max volume, otherwise the aeration will be limiting.

To prep for Day 3:

Chill the following in 4C fridge:
• Centrifuge rotor (ask Hackspace)
• 1L 10% glycerol

Day 3:

1. In a flask, inoculate 2.5mL of the starter culture in 250mL fresh SOB medium (the volumes of starter culture and SOB should always be at a 1:100 ratio).

2. Incubate at 37C in a rotary shaker set to 225rpm until the OD600 is between 0.4-0.6 (0.4 most common).

NOTE: Optimal transformation efficiencies are achieved when E. coli cells are at an optical density, OD600nm ~0.4-0.5. DO NOT LET IT GO ABOVE 0.5. To obtain this biomass, the cell suspension should be checked every 30 minutes using a spectrophotometer. It usually takes between 2-3 hours after inoculation of the culture to reach this point. There is a 10 minute window to transfer the flask to ice (see next step).

3. Once target OD600 is hit, transfer flask to ice, then transfer all contents of the flask to a 4x fresh 50mL Falcon tubes and keep on ice for 5-10 min. Swirl occasionally to ensure that cooling occurs evenly.

NOTE: After this point, do not let cells warm up past 4C. When pouring off supernatant, be careful not to disturb the pellet. If the pellet seems loose, re-spin the cells down. Aspirate with a pipette if necessary.

4. Centrifuge 1: Pellet the cells by centrifuging at 4000rpm for 5 minutes at 4C.

5. Pour off supernatant. Aspirate any residual broth. Resuspend each Falcon tube pellet in 10mL ice-cold sterile distilled water, then add another 40mL (so total = 50mL water added per Falcon).

6. Centrifuge 2: Pellet the cells again by centrifuging at 4000rpm for 5 minutes at 4C.

7. Resuspend in 2x 50mL distilled water (start with 4 tubes, resuspend each pellet in 10mL water, then transfer the contents of Tube 1 to Tube 2 and the contents of Tube 3 to Tube 4, then add 30mL distilled water to each of the remaining 2 tubes).

NOTE: At the end of this process, you should have 2 empty Falcons, and 2 full Falcons.

8. Centrifuge 3: Pellet the cells again by centrifuging at 4000rpm for 5 minutes at 4C.

9. Resuspend in 2x 50mL 10% glycerol.

10. Centrifuge 4: Pellet the cells again by centrifuging at 4000rpm for 7 minutes at 4C.

11. Pour away supernatant slowly, then resuspend the cells in a final volume of 200uL of ice-cold 10% glycerol.

NOTE: At this stage, you should have a total volume of 400uL cell suspension, divided into 2x 200uL in Falcons. At this stage, either move onto electroporation protocol or store away.

12. Snap-freeze: Pre-cool 10x 1.5mL eppys on dry ice, keep lids open. Aliquot 40uL cells into each tube, work quickly. Store in –80C freezer.

DESCRIPTION:

MATERIALS AND APPARATUS:

• Electrocompetent TOP10 cells stored in –80C freezer
• Plasmid to be transformed into cells
• Ice bucket
• LB-Chlor plates for selection
• Graduated cylinder
• Weighing Scale/Boats (check mg scale works)
• Microcentrifuge tubes (1.5mL eppys) & 0.1cm electroporation cuvettes
o Quantity: Each sample to be electroporated needs one fresh 1.5mL microcentrifuge tube and one 0.1cm cuvette.
• Hackspace-provided Eppendorf 5810R Centrifuge
• LB-Chlor liquid broth
• 10% (v/v) glycerol: 12.6g glycerol in 90mL of distilled water. Autoclave or filter sterilise.
• SOC recovery medium

LB broth/agar:

Per 500mL LB agar: (25 plates)

• 2.5g yeast extract
• 5g peptone
• 5g NaCl
• 7.5g agar-agar
• 1L sterile bottle (check to see if this fits in the microwave for reheating)
• Ice bucket for keeping antibiotic on
• Chloroamphenicol antibiotic stored at 1000X concentration, dissolved in EtOH
o Recommended stock conc = 25mg/mL, working concentration = 25ug/mL

Procedure: per 500mL molten LB agar = 25 plates

NOTE: This makes 25 plates, but we only need 16 plates total for Stage 1.

1. Weigh out the following into a 1L sterile bottle (20g total, pre-mixed powder in Hackspace contains first three components):
• 2.5g yeast extract
• 5g peptone
• 5g NaCl

If making LB agar plates, add:
• 7.5g agar-agar

2. Make up solution to 500mL using sterile dH2O, gently swirl to mix and minimise amount of powder left on sides of bottle.

3. Screw lid on bottle loosely and label with autoclave tape

4. Autoclave at 121C for 20 minutes

5. Remove solution from autoclave and cool to <50C (just about hot to touch but not too hot).

NOTE: Don’t need to use water bath for cooling to specific temperature unless generating a lot of plates & need to stop the agar from solidifying.

6. If making LB-Chlor plates, cool the LB medium down further until near room temperature and add 500uL of antibiotic to solution and swirl to mix.

SOC (Super Optimised Broth with Catabolite Repression)

Per litre of SOB:

NOTE: If available in dry premixed form in the Hackspace, check ratios of each component.

• 2% (w/v) tryptone/peptone
• 0.5% (w/v) yeast extract
• 10 mM NaCl
• 2.5 mM KCl
• 20 mM MgSO4

1. Dissolve the following in 500mL of distilled water (ddH2O):
• 10g tryptone/peptone
• 5g yeast extract
• 0.292g NaCl
• 0.093g KCl
• 1.2g MgSO4

2. Bring final volume to 500mL with more distilled water (ddH2O)

Adjust to pH 7.5 before use, requires approx. 25mL of 1M NaOH solution per litre of SOB made (so instead of using 200mL water to bring final volume to 1L, use 175mL water + 25mL 1M NaOH.)

3. Cool SOB medium to <50C, then add 10ml syringe filter-sterilised 20% (w/v) glucose solution

NOTE: To filter-sterilise the glucose solution, attach a single-use, sterile 0.2um pore filter to a syringe tip, and screw down the syringe to secure it. Position the syringe at the mouth of a sterile Falcon tube, then pour the glucose solution into the syringe and gently push down the plunger.

Preparation steps for previous day:
• Pre-warm 2mL SOC recovery medium and LB-Chlor agar plates in 37C incubator (no shaking necessary) for at least 1 hour.
• Make 50% glycerol

NOTE: For each 40uL aliquot of electrocompetent cells, use 2mL SOC. Use this ratio to scale up for each transformation reaction you want to do. # cells in aliquot doesn’t matter, colonies will be grown out of them anyway.

• Pre-chill in 4C freezer: plasmid DNA, electroporation cuvettes, microcentrifuge tubes, distilled water, and 10% glycerol.

PROCEDURE:

> 1. Thaw the 40uL aliquots of electrocompetent cells on ice. Add 2 µL of purified DNA from Miniprep.

2. Mix by gently flicking the tube containing the electrocompetent cell + DNA mixture. Do not handle cells vigorously as this will reduce transformation efficiency. Let the mixture sit on ice for 1 minute.

3. Pipette the mixture into a chilled 0.1 cm electroporation cuvette, gently tap the cuvette on a flat surface to ensure the mixture is at the bottom of the cuvette.

4. Wipe any condensation off the sides of the cuvette before placing the cuvette in the chamber slide.

5. Push the slide into the chamber until the cuvette is seated between the contacts in the base of the chamber. Ensure the pulser is set to “Ec1” and press the "Pulse" button once.

6. Remove the cuvette from the chamber and immediately add 1mL of pre-warmed SOC recovery medium to the cuvette. Quickly and gently resuspend cells with a pipette.

NOTE: you need to be quick about transferring the cells to SOC medium; delaying the transfer between the stage at which you apply the pulse to the stage where SOC medium is added is crucial for recovering transformants. Delaying by a single minute causes a 3-fold drop in transformation.

7. Transfer the mixture to a new 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.

8. Incubate for 60 minutes at 37°C in a shaking incubator at 225rpm – ensure the tube lays flat.

9. Prepare two plates:
a. Streak plate 50uL of the cell mixture on a LB-Chlor plate. Incubate overnight at 37C.
b. Spin the remainder of cells at 3000 rpm for 2 minutes. Remove all but 50 μL media (otherwise you will lose pellet). Streak plate with remaining cells on a LB-Chlor plate. Incubate overnight at 37°C.

Making glycerol stocks of transformed cells:

1. Prepare autoclaved liquid LB-Chlor medium. Reserve 10mL of LB-Chlor for blank when measuring OD600.

2. Making an overnight culture: pick a colony of transformed cells from the LB-Chlor plate and add to 5mL LB-Chlor in 50mL Falcon (for mini-prep of plasmid). Incubate the tube overnight at 37C at 225rpm.

3. Next morning, take 2 ml for miniprep, use remaining for glycerol stock.

4. Add 1 mL of the overnight culture to an equal amount of 50% filtered glycerol in a 2 mL screw top tube or Eppys and gently mix by pipetting 5-6 times.

Note: Adding the 50% glycerol to an equal volume of cells effectively dilutes it down to 25%

5. Freeze the glycerol stock tube at -80°C. Subsequent freeze and thaw cycles reduce shelf life.

6. To recover bacteria from your glycerol stock, open the tube and use a sterile loop, toothpick or pipette tip to scrape some of the frozen bacteria off the top. Do not let the glycerol stock unthaw. Keep on ice block.

7. Grow bacteria overnight at 37C, for plasmid miniprep the following day.

Sources: Horizons prototyping + electroporation machine manual, Addgene glycerol stock page.

DESCRIPTION:

Background: all QIAGEN plasmid purification protocols are based on a modified alkaline lysis procedure, followed by binding plasmid DNA to resin under low-salt and pH conditions. A medium-salt wash is then conducted to remove RNA, proteins, dyes, and other low-MW contaminants. The plasmid DNA is then eluted in a high-salt buffer and concentrated and desalted by isopropanol precipitation.

NOTE: Protocol is optimised for use with cultures grown to cell density of approximately 3-4 x 10^9 cells per mL. Ensure OD600 = 0.5.

MATERIALS AND APPARATUS:

> • Ice
• Isopropanol
• Ethanol, 70%
• Plasmid resuspension buffer (e.g., TE buffer, pH 8.0, or Tris·Cl, pH 8.5 - check if we have any...)
• Tabletop microcentrifuge
• 1.5mL Eppy tubes

MATERIALS IN THE MINIKIT (12125):

• x100 QIAGEN-tip 20
• 2 x 20 ml Buffer P1
• 2 x 20 ml Buffer P2
• 2 x 20 ml Buffer P3
• 2 x 60 ml Buffer QBT
• 2 x 240 ml Buffer QC
• 140 ml Buffer QF
• 2 x 2 mg RNase A (check if 10 mg/ml or 100 mg/ml solution)
• 2 x 20 ul LyseBlue

Prep steps:

1. Centrifuge RNase A briefly, then add the whole bottle into Buffer P1 (which gives a final concentration of 100 ug/mL). Then add the whole bottle of LyseBlue into Buffer P1.

2. Check Buffer P2 for SDS precipitation

3. Prechill (one bottle of) Buffer P3 at 4°C

4. Make ice

5. Chill centrifuge

PROCEDURE:

1. Grow cells to reach a cell density of approximately 3–4 x 109 cells per milliliter. (For 1 ml of cells, OD = 5; for 5 ml of cells, OD =~1)

- NOTE: Use a tube or flask with a volume of at least 4 times the volume of the culture for these:

- Pick a single colony to inoculate in a 3mL LB medium starter culture containing 25ug/uL chloramphenicol. Incubate for 8h at 37°C in a rotary shaker at 300 rpm.

2. Centrifuge cells at 6000 x g for 15 min at 4°C.

NOTE: If you wish to stop the protocol and continue later, freeze the cell pellets at −30 to −15°C.

3. Resuspend the bacterial pellet in 0.3 ml of Buffer P1 (ensure RNase A has been already added) in a 2 ml Eppy (for centrifuging).

4. Add 0.3 ml of Buffer P2, mix thoroughly by vigorously inverting the sealed tube 4−6 times, and incubate at room temperature for 5 min.

NOTE: Do not vortex. Do not leave solution for longer than 5mins. Close buffer P2 quickly (to avoid CO2 reacting with solution).

5. Add 0.3 ml of (pre-chilled) Buffer P3, mix immediately and thoroughly by vigorously inverting 4–6 times, and incubate on ice for 5 min.

NOTE: After addition of Buffer P3, a fluffy white material forms (contains proteins/DNA etc) and the lysate becomes less viscous. If the mixture still appears viscous, more mixing is required to completely neutralize the solution.

6. Mix the sample (by inverting). Centrifuge at maximum speed in a microcentrifuge for 10 min.

7. (while centrifuge runs) set up a QIAGEN-tip 20 on a tube rack with a waste beaker under the column to collect the flow-through. Add 1ml of Buffer QBT (equilibration buffer) into the QIAGEN-tip 20, allow it to flow through the column completely before pouring in the supernatant in the next step.

NOTE: Try not to let the column dry out

8. (once centrifuge is finished) keep the supernatant containing plasmid DNA – pour it into the column quickly. The supernatant (containing plasmid DNA) will be clear, the precipitate (containing genomic GNA) will be white.

NOTE: After centrifugation, the supernatant should be clear - centrifuge again if needed.

9. Add 2 ml Buffer QC into the QIAGEN-tip 20 and let flow through. Then add again 2 ml Buffer QC into the QIAGEN-tip 20 and let flow through.

10. Remove waste beaker from under the column and replace with 1.5 ml Eppy. Add 0.8 ml Buffer QF to elute the plasmid DNA into the Eppy.

11. Take 2 µL of the elute and use Nanodrop machine (ds DNA setting) to quantify the amount (ideally around 50 ng per µL of DNA), and get a purity score and quality score (ideally above 1.8-2.1). Blank first with 2 µL of elution buffer.

Complete following steps if scores are below 1.8-2.1 or if concentration is below 10 ng per µL. (Because the Nanodrop cannot accurately read concentrations below 10 ng per µL).

12. Precipitate DNA by adding 0.7 volumes (e.g. 0.56 ml per 0.8 ml of elution volume) of room-temperature isopropanol to the eluted DNA. Mix and centrifuge immediately at ≥15,000 x g rpm for 30 min in a microcentrifuge. Remove the supernatant using a pipette (without touching the pellet).

NOTES: All solutions should be at room temperature to minimize salt precipitation. Isopropanol pellets have a glassy appearance and may be more difficult to see than the fluffy, salt-containing pellets that result from ethanol precipitation. Marking the outside of the tube before centrifugation allows the pellet to be easily located. Isopropanol pellets are more loosely attached to the side of the tube; take care when removing the supernatant.

13. Wash DNA pellet with 1 ml of 70% ethanol and centrifuge at 15,000 x g for 10 min. Carefully decant the supernatant without disturbing the pellet.

14. Open the tube, gently blot off excess ethanol, and leave to air-dry the pellet for 5–10 min, and redissolve the DNA in a suitable volume (0.2 - 0.5 ml) of elution buffer.

15. Re-measure DNA quantity using Nanodrop (purity/quality scores should be ideally above 1.8-2.1), and concentration should be around 50 ng per µL.

2 µL of the purified DNA will be used for each round of electroporation. Any remaining DNA can be stored at room temperature.

Troubleshooting: take samples at step 7, 9, 10 and 11 to run agarose gel analysis and see where the problem occurred.
